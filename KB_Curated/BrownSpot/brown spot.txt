Brown Spot Disease

Bangla Name
Badami Dag Rog

Introduction
Brown spot is a fungal disease of rice. It occurs in all seasons and can attack different parts of the rice plant, though it is most commonly seen on the leaves and seeds. The severity of the disease increases when the soil lacks nutrients or water.

Visual Symptoms
The leaves have small round or oval brown or dark-brown spots surrounded by a yellow halo.
Large spots or lesions of the leaves have grayish or light-brown or dark-brown surrounded by a yellow halo.

Symptoms Details
a) On Leaves:
1) Small brown spots first appear on the leaves.
2) These spots gradually enlarge, becoming somewhat similar in size to sesame seeds.
3) The size and color of the spots vary depending on the rice variety.
4) In susceptible varieties, the spots grow larger, with the center turning grayish or light brown. Numerous spots may form, causing the leaf to die.
5) Sometimes, a yellow halo can be seen around the lesions.

b) On Seeds:
1) Infected seeds show light to dark brown spots on their surface.
2) In most cases, the grains become smaller than normal.
3) Under favorable weather conditions, the infection can become severe, turning the entire seed brown; even the polished rice may show brown spots.
4) Sometimes the fungus can be seen growing on the seed surface, covering the grain with fungal mycelia.
5) When infected seeds germinate, the fungus attacks the roots and coleoptile, causing distinct dark-brown lesions on them, which may lead to seedling death.

Favorable Conditions for Disease Development
1) Light soil with low water-holding capacity.
2) Deficiency of organic matter in the soil.
3) Use of infected seeds.
4) Lack of nitrogen, potash, zinc fertilizers, and inadequate water supply.

Management Practices
1) Use balanced fertilizers.
2) Apply a high amount of organic manure.
3) Maintain proper water management.
4) Use healthy, disease-free seeds.
5) Treat seeds with Bavistin or Nativo (3 g per kg of seed).
6) Apply fertilizers in proper recommended doses.
